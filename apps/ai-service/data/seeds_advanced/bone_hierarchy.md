# Mechanism: Bone Hierarchy (Structural Optimization)

## Biological Context
Mammalian bone is a highly optimized structural material that provides strength and flexibility while remaining lightweight.

## Physical Principle
- **Hierarchical Scaling**: Bone is organized across seven levels of hierarchy, from the molecular (collagen/hydroxyapatite) to the macroscopic (trabecular and cortical bone).
- **Trabecular (Spongy) Architecture**: At the interior, the bone has a porous, lattice-like structure that is aligned with the primary stress lines (Wolff's Law). This maximizes strength where needed while shedding mass where load is minimal.
- **Cortical (Compact) Shielding**: The outer shell is dense and provides high stiffness and protection against impact.
- **Toughness through Complexity**: The interfaces between these layers (e.g., the cement line) prevent cracks from propagating easily, making the entire structure remarkably resistant to fracture.

## Engineering Applications
- **3D Printed Lattice Structures**: Designing lightweight aerospace components using generative design principles that mimic trabecular bone.
- **Sustainable Architecture**: Planning bridge and building trusses that use material only where stress is highest, reducing concrete and steel waste.
- **Automotive Lightweighting**: Developing hollow, graded-density frame components for electric vehicles to extend range without sacrificing crash safety.

## Keywords
Wolff's Law, Trabecular Bone, Structural Hierarchy, Lightweighting, Optimization, Generative Design.
